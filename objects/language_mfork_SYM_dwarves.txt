language_SYM_dwarves

[OBJECT:LANGUAGE]

Vanilla: +ARTIFICE +EARTH -DOMESTIC -SUBORDINATE -EVIL -FLOWERY -NEGATIVE -UGLY -NEGATOR
Dwarves have a stereotypical naming scheme in fantasy fiction: "Thorin Oakenshield" from "Dwarrowdelf" (LotR) and "Bruenor Battlehammer" (D&D) being notables among them. There's also plenty of named dwarves in Norse folklore, with the Poetic Edda citing many. Since these symbols are also inextricably used for site compound names, geonyms also feature prominently among names.
Many of the conventions established here, such as "minor" civ adjectives or "profession"-themed names also extend to the other naming conventions. The tags <, V, and > denote what compound positions a word can be used in, with V being the unpredictable STANDARD_VERB case. Double-verbs often look silly, so I've taken care to generally limit them to the main symbol.
Squads don't use the MILITARY_UNIT symbol, but I've defined them for all of the namelists in case they start to be used.
